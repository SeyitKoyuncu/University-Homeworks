Iskembe Soup
  • 500 gr sheep or beef tripe
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 10 glasses of water
  • 10 glasses of water
  • 2 cups of milk
  • 1 tablespoon of flour
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  1. Add enough water to cover 1 kg of well-cleaned tripe and boil it for about 40 minutes until the tripe is soft.
  2. Melt 3 tablespoons of butter in a separate pan, add 3 tablespoons of flour and fry until the smell of flour is gone.
  3. Add the strained and warmed boiling water to the pan little by little. To prevent the flour from clumping; Stir quickly with a whisk.
  4. Slowly feed this mixture into the pot where you boiled the tripe, stirring constantly.
  5. To prepare the seasoning of the soup; Whisk 4 tablespoons of yogurt, 2 tablespoons of flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, 1 egg yolk and juice of half a lemon in a separate bowl.
  6. After running the dressing mix with boiling boiling water, add it little by little to the soup pot and mix it quickly.
  7. Finally; Add the boiled tripe to the soup and cook on low heat by boiling for 15 minutes.
  8. After grating the garlic, mix it with vinegar. Serve the soup you bought in serving bowls hot with the addition of garlic vinegar. Enjoy your meal!

Chicken Vegetable Kebab
  • one piece onion
  • one piece potato
  • 3 capsicum peppers
  • 3 green chili peppers
  • 2 garlic clove
  • one piece chicken breast
  • 1/3 teaspoon of olive oil
  • some spices
  1. Wash all vegetables. Remove the seeds from the peppers. Chop the vegetables into cubes of equal size.
  2. First cut the chicken breast in half horizontally. Then dice finely.
  3. Mix vegetables and chicken. Add all the spices.
  4. Mix olive oil, water and tomato paste in a bowl. When all the ingredients are combined, add them to the chicken mortar and mix.
  5. Spread the mixture you have prepared in a flat baking mold.
  6. Cut your wax paper suitable for the size of your mold and place it on it. Bake in a preheated 180 degree fan oven for 25-30 minutes with greaseproof paper on it.
  7. Then remove it from the greaseproof paper and continue to bake in the oven for 5-10 minutes with the top open. Serve hot after taking it out of the oven, bon appetit!

Humus
  • 2 cups chickpeas
  • 2 garlic clove
  • 2 piece lemon
  • 1/2 cup of tahini
  • 1/2 teaspoon of olive oil
  • some spices
  1. Soak the chickpeas overnight or at least 4-5 hours beforehand. Add 1 teaspoon of baking soda to its water to facilitate digestion and to cook more easily. The next day, drain the water and rinse again.
  2. Put the chickpeas in the pot, add water and leave to cook. It will cook for 45 minutes in a normal steel pot. Once the chickpeas have softened, remove them from the heat. Set aside 1-2 tablespoons to serve on hummus.
  3. Meanwhile, put the tahini, olive oil, cumin and salt in a bowl. Add the juice of the lemons as well. Finally, finely chop the garlic, add them and mix well.
  4. After boiling, drain the chickpeas and grind them with the tahini mixture you prepared in the food processor until they get a smooth consistency. If the food processor is struggling, add olive oil or water little by little to reach the desired consistency.
  5. Then take it on a serving plate and draw a circle in the middle with a dessert spoon.
  6. Sprinkle 1-2 tablespoons of chickpeas that you have set aside.
  7. Finally, drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with ground pepper and serve. Enjoy your meal!
